Does global warming cause breathing problems?

63 findings from 7 sources

Consensus Analysis

Yes, global warming causes breathing problems through multiple pathways: increased ground-level ozone formation that triggers respiratory disease, more frequent wildfires producing harmful smoke and particulate matter, and increased allergens that worsen asthma. Climate change also indirectly affects respiratory health by increasing heat-related hospital admissions for respiratory diseases.
